BudgetView organizes your budget in five areas:

Income

This area covers all your income sources, recurring or not.

The most frequent types of income are salaries, revenue from freelance work, author fees, and also revenue from investments.

\

Recurring expenses

You will find in this area all expenditures that occur periodically - every month, every two or six months, or every year.

The most common kinds of recurring expenses are subscriptions, rent, loans, etc.

\

Variable expenses

Variable expenses change from one month to the other depending on your consumption. Most of the time a variable expenses envelope will have several transactions in a given month, whereas a recurring expenses envelope will have only one.

The most common examples of variable expenses are groceries, leisures, health, clothing, etc.

Savings

This area covers all the transactions made on your savings accounts.

Extras

You use the "Extras" area for exceptional transactions - important purchases, holidays and trips, unexpected expenditures, etc.

Your projects appear as "Extras" envelopes.
