Managing a family budget

Do you have a clear picture of your finances? Are you managing your accounts in the best way to master your spending and accomplish your most important financial goals?

If you're like us and most of our friends, you glance at your bank accounts rarely and then only to check for suspicious transactions or to avoid overdrawing funds.

For years, both of us followed this approach before discovering its limits: as the months passed, and despite salary increases, we ended up with nothing left in our accounts at the end of each month. As a result, we couldn't achieve those goals that were important to us: buying a house, going on trips, etc.

Where was our money going? It was very difficult to know for certain.

To tell the truth, managing financial accounts has never been fun or even easy. It's difficult to make sense out of any pattern in the long transaction statements that the bank sends out every few months or displays on its website. Most money management software is no better since it is still based on the same tedious lists.

A few years ago, we discovered a solution to this problem: a clear picture of our budget and the anticipation of how it would unfold in the coming weeks and months would allow us to master our spending instead of just suffering its consequences.

To fulfill this, we started two complementary experiments:

  • We began to design and develop the BudgetView application, which would allow us to have a clear vision of our budget without the tedious management of long lists of financial operations.
  • We read books, browsed articles, and talked to those around us to find the main principles of personal financial management.

Here is what we learned:

Start with the vision of your goals

If you're not trying to reach a specific goal that is important to you, why accept more restrictions on your spending than you need?

Think about your own projects. What would you like to do? Maybe you'd like to go on a trip to the end of the world? Perhaps you want to enjoy comfort at home by installing a jacuzzi in your basement or buying the latest home cinema installation. Or maybe you want to save your hard-earned money so that you can face any unexpected situations in the future, prepare for retirement, or pay for your daughter's college education.

Take the time to think about it seriously and to talk it over with your spouse. After all, why spend time managing your budget if you have no goal?

State your goals clearly

From a budgeting point of view, you should be able to define your goal as follows:

“In ___ months, I need _____ to ___________________________”

Using this formula, divide the desired funds by the number of months you have planned. The result is the amount of money you need to save each month in order to reach that goal.

Here are a few examples:

“In 18 months, I need 3600$ for my trip across Argentina.”

200$/month

“In 6 months, I need 900$ to replace the old furniture in my kitchen."

150$/month

“In 12 months, I need 1000$ in savings to be able to face any unexpected event.”

85$/month

What are your ideal goals?

Pay yourself first!

After you have calculated the monthly sum needed to achieve your goal, the most efficient way to save the money is to ask your bank to automatically transfer that amount to your savings at the beginning of each month. We recommend that you open a savings account if you don't already have one.

Putting your money in a "safe place" like this will help you fight the temptation to spend it! You can then start managing your budget more accurately and efficiently.

Master your budget

The main consequence of this automatic transfer is that your budget will now be a little tighter. You will have to monitor it even closer to avoid any overdrafts at the end of the month.

This is where BudgetView shines. To avoid being in the red, you start each month by setting up the envelopes that will track the revenue and spending of your budget: salary, groceries, leisure, etc.

You then set planned amounts for each envelope so that the balance of your accounts never becomes negative when the application computes your estimated budget:

After setting up the envelopes, you will need to check at least once a week that your budget stays in line with your plans. If you encounter a cost overrun in one envelope, you can adjust the amounts in other envelopes to avoid any overdraw. This allows you to stay within budget without compromising the money you have set aside for your goals.

After a few weeks, when you have become comfortable with the process of monitoring your budget, you can then begin planning for the next few months to allow the program to project the evolution of your finances.

Voilà! You are now on track to realize your projects!
